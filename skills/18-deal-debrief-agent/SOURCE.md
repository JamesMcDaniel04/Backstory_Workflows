# Deal Debrief Agent

## Description
Post-close analysis for won or lost deals — what worked, what didn't, decisive stakeholders, competitive dynamics, and lessons learned for institutional knowledge.

## Audience
AEs, Sales managers, Sales leaders

## Input
Account name (selects a closed opportunity)

## MCP Tools Used
- find_account
- get_opportunity_status
- get_recent_opportunity_activity
- ask_sales_ai_about_opportunity
- get_account_status
- get_engaged_people

## Project Knowledge Files
- None

**Deal Timeline**

---

**What Worked**

- **Champion enablement:** @lisa.park was armed with an internal business case deck tailored to ACME's metrics. She presented it to @robert.tanaka without us in the room — and it landed.
- **Technical proof-of-concept:** The 2-week POC with @maria.santos eliminated all technical objections before they reached the executive conversation.
- **Exec-to-exec alignment:** Bringing in @mark.sullivan at the right moment (not too early) gave @james.wright confidence in the partnership.
- **Competitive positioning:** Focused on integration depth vs. Rival.io's breadth. ACME's #1 pain was consolidation — we won on that axis.

---

**Key Moments**

- **Turning point:** The bake-off results in January. @maria.santos reported 40% faster API response times vs. Rival.io. This became the internal talking point.
- **Risk moment:** February budget pushback from @robert.tanaka. Deal nearly stalled. Recovered by providing a 3-year TCO analysis showing ===$230,000=== savings vs. incumbent multi-vendor stack.
- **Close trigger:** @james.wright's board presentation in May required a vendor decision. Created natural urgency without artificial pressure.

---

**Engagement Analysis**

- **Total meetings:** 23 over 8 months
- **Stakeholders engaged:** 6 (4 active in final month)
- **Email threads:** 47 | Avg. response time from ACME: 4.2 hours
- **Champion activity:** @lisa.park attended 19 of 23 meetings and sent 3 internal advocacy emails (surfaced via CRM signals)
- **Executive engagement:** @robert.tanaka joined 2 meetings — both were decisive

---

**Lessons Learned**

- **Do again:** Build the internal business case deck *for* the champion. @lisa.park said it was the single most useful asset we provided.
- **Do again:** Run the POC early. Technical validation before exec conversations shortened the negotiation phase.
- **Improve:** Anticipate legal/procurement friction earlier. The 2-week stall on data residency was avoidable with a pre-approved clause library.
- **Improve:** Engage @robert.tanaka one meeting earlier. The budget surprise in February added unnecessary risk to the timeline.
