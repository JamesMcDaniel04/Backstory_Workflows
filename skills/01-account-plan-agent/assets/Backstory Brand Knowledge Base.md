## Logo

[cite_start]The Backstory brand utilizes a logotype, a logomark, and a social mark[cite: 10, 11].

* [cite_start]**Logotype**: This is the full word-mark "backstory"[cite: 10]. [cite_start]It should be used wherever possible[cite: 11].
* [cite_start]**Logomark**: This consists of the "p." element of the logo[cite: 10].
* [cite_start]**Social Mark**: This is an optically aligned version of the logomark for use in social media contexts[cite: 10].

### Construction and Spacing

* [cite_start]The word-mark's letter-forms have a consistent width (1y)[cite: 9].
* [cite_start]A key design feature is the 36Â° angle in the dot "ramp," which is also present in the "p" letter-forms[cite: 10]. [cite_start]This angle and the spacing around it should not be altered[cite: 10].
* [cite_start]The required clear space around the logotype and logomark is equal to the x-height of the letter-forms (1x)[cite: 10].

### Usage on Backgrounds

[cite_start]The logo can be used on various colored backgrounds, including white, light blue, and dark blue, to ensure visibility and brand consistency[cite: 13].

## Typography

[cite_start]The brand's typography is crucial for maintaining a consistent visual identity[cite: 28].

* [cite_start]**Primary Typeface**: Proxima Nova Bold, used for headers[cite: 27, 29].
* [cite_start]**Secondary Typeface**: Proxima Nova Regular, used for body copy[cite: 27, 30].

[cite_start]The Proxima Nova family includes various weights such as Light, Regular, Semibold, Bold, and Black[cite: 29, 30].
